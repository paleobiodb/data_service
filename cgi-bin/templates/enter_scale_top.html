Comments:
  • Your current reference will become the time scale's reference.
  • This form is for scales, not charts. Enter each scale in a chart separately; enter epochs separately from stages, stages separately from zones, etc.
  • Always enter coarser scales first: epochs before stages before zones, etc.
  • Make separate entries for Early/Lower, Middle, and Late/Upper subdivisions if and only if these subdivisions are shown in the published time scale. Subdivisions may be formal or informal.
  • Never enter "Early," "Middle," "Late," "Lower," or "Upper" into the interval or correlate name fields. Use the pulldowns instead!
  • The only required field is interval name, but maximum and minimum correlates are strongly recommended.
  • Maximum and minimum correlates are intervals from a different scale, e.g., epochs if you are entering stages. The correlates should be coarser than, or at least as coarse as, the intervals of the new scale you are entering. Don't map coarse intervals into fine intervals! Multiple intervals can map into one correlate.
