Major individual data sets reposited in the Paleobiology Database

These data sets are considered official electronic publications of the Paleobiology Database. Data sets are listed alphabetically by authorizer.

  • North American Mammalian Paleofaunal Database 2002
    • Authorizer: John Alroy
    • Enterer: John Alroy
    • Primary reference: J. Alroy. 1996. Constant extinction, constrained diversification, and uncoordinated stasis in North American mammals. Palaeogeography, Palaeoclimatology, Palaeoecology 127(1/4):285-311.
    • To download the data: Search for authorizer = J. Alroy and time interval = Cretaceous or Tertiary and research group = vertebrate
  • Middle Devonian fossils of the Michigan Basin
    • Authorizer: Brian Bodenbender
    • Enterer: Erin Wysocki
    • Primary reference: C. A. Ferguson, B. E. Bodenbender, J. L. Jones, and K. Ahmed. 2000. Recording the fossil record: a GIS database of Middle Devonian fossils in the Michigan Basin. Geological Society of America, Abstracts with Programs 32(7):A131.
    • To download the data: Search for authorizer = B. Bodenbender and time interval = Devonian
  • Taxonomy and distribution of Late Jurassic - Eocene lissamphibians
    • Authorizer: Emmanuel Fara
    • Enterer: Emmanuel Fara
    • Primary reference: E. Fara. 2001. Macroevolutionary patterns and the quality of the fossil record: from data to processes. Ph.D. Thesis, University of Bristol, Bristol, UK
    • To download the data (Excel spreadsheet): click here
  • Jurassic marine faunas of France, Greenland, Portugal, and the United Kingdom
    • Authorizer: Franz Fürsich
    • Enterers: Hans Eichiner, Franz Fürsich, Danica Hempfling, Elmar Link
    • To download the data: Search for authorizer = F. Fursich and time interval = Jurassic
  • Silurian and Early Devonian plants 2001
    • Authorizer: Pat Gensel
    • Enterers: Michele Kotyk, Allison Tuell
    • To download the data: Search for authorizer = P. Gensel and time interval = Silurian or Devonian
  • Ivany Thesis Collection: Middle Eocene US Gulf Coast Macrofossils
    • Authorizer: Linda Ivany
    • Enterer: Linda Ivany
    • To download the data: Search for authorizer = L. Ivany and time interval = Eocene
  • Maastrichtian bivalve faunas of the world 1994
    • Authorizer: David Jablonski
    • Enterers: John Alroy, David Jablonski
    • Primary reference: D. M. Raup and D. Jablonski. 1993. Geography of end-Cretaceous marine bivalve extinctions. Science 260:971-973.
    • To download the data: Search for authorizer = D. Jablonski and time interval = Cretaceous
  • Marine Bivalve Genera, Revision of Sepkoski's Compendium
    • Authorizer: David Jablonski
    • Enterers: David Jablonski, Kaustuv Roy, James Valentine, Rebecca Price, Philip Anderson
    • Primary reference: D. Jablonski, K. Roy, J. W. Valentine, RF. M. Price, and P. S. Anderson. 2003. The impact of the Pull of the Recent on the history of bivalve diversity. Science 300:1133-1135.
    • To download the data (Excel spreadsheet): click here
  • Upper Cretaceous larger invertebrate fossils from the Haustator bilira zone of the Atlantic and Gulf Coastal Plains
    • Authorizer: Matt Kosnik
    • Enterer: Matt Kosnik
    • Primary references:
      N. F. Sohl and C. F. Koch. 1983. Upper Cretaceous (Maestrichtian) Mollusca from the Haustator bilira Assemblage Zone in the East Gulf Coastal Plain. USGS Open File Report 83-451:1-239
      N. F. Sohl and C. F. Koch. 1984. Upper Cretaceous (Maestrichtian) larger invertebrate Fossils from the Haustator bilira Assemblage Zone in the west gulf Coastal Plain. USGS Open File Report 84-687:1-282
      N. F. Sohl and C. F. Koch. 1987. Upper Cretaceous (Maestrichtian) larger invertebrates from the Haustator bilira Assemblage Zone in the Atlantic Coastal Plain with further data for the East Gulf. USGS Open File Report 87-194:1-172
    • To download the data: Search for authorizer = M. Kosnik and stage= Maastrichtian
  • Ordovician marine faunas of the world
    • Authorizer: Arnie Miller
    • Enterers: Phil Novack-Gottshall, Rich Krause
    • Primary reference: A. I. Miller. 1997. Dissecting Global Diversity Patterns: examples from the Ordovician Radiation. Annual Review of Ecology and Systematics 28:85-104.
    • To download the data: Search for authorizer = A. Miller and time interval = Ordovician
  • Pennsylvanian-Permian Marine Benthos of the North American Midcontinent
    • Authorizer: Tom Olszewski
    • Enterers: Melissa Gibson, Katrina Nell, Tom Olszewski
    • Primary reference: T. D. Olszewski and M. E. Patzkowsky. 2001, Measuring recurrence of marine biotic gradients: A case study from the Pennsylvanian-Permian Midcontinent. PALAIOS 16:444-460.
    • To download the data: Search for authorizer = T. Olszewski and time interval = Carboniferous or Permian
  • PGAP's Permian, Triassic and Jurassic terrestrial megafloras of the world
    • Authorizer: A. Rees
    • Enterer: A. Rees
    • Note: Data are from the Paleogeographic Atlas Project (PGAP), led by Fred Ziegler (University of Chicago)
    • Primary references:
      A.M. Ziegler. 1990. Phytogeographic patterns and continental configurations during the Permian Period. Pp. 363-379 in W.S. McKerrow and C.R. Scotese, Eds., Palaeozoic Palaeogeography and Biogeography. Geological Society of London Memoir 12.
      A.M. Ziegler, J.M. Parrish, J. Yao, E.D. Gyllenhaal, D.B. Rowley, J.T. Parrish, S. Nie, A. Bekker and M.L. Hulver. 1994. Early Mesozoic phytogeography and climate. Pp. 89-97 in J.R.L. Allen, B.J. Hoskins, B.W. Sellwood, R.A. Spicer and P.J. Valdes, Eds., Palaeoclimates and their modelling; with special reference to the Mesozoic era. Chapman and Hall , London.
      P.M. Rees. 2002. Land-plant diversity and the end-Permian mass extinction. Geology 30:827-830.
    • To download the data: Search for authorizer = A. Rees and time interval = Permian, Triassic, or Jurassic
  • Paleozoic marine faunas from the paleocontinent of Laurentia 1992
    • Authorizer: J. John Sepkoski, Jr.
    • Enterer: Mike Sommers
    • Primary reference: J. J. Sepkoski, Jr., and A. I. Miller. 1985. Evolutionary faunas and the distribution of Paleozoic benthic communities in space and time. Pp. 153-190 in J. W. Valentine, Ed., Phanerozoic Diversity Patterns: Profiles in Macroevolution. Princeton University Press, Princeton, New Jersey.
    • To download the data: Search for authorizer = J. Sepkoski
  • Carboniferous terrestrial floras of North America and Europe
    • Authorizer: Hallie Sims
    • Enterers: Heather Lindon, Summer Ostrowski
    • To download the data: Search for authorizer = H. Sims and time interval = Carboniferous
  • Paleogene Cetacea of the World
    • Authorizer: Mark Uhen
    • Enterer: Mark Uhen
    • To download the data: Search for oldest time interval = Eocene and youngest time interval = Oligocene and taxon = Cetacea
  • Neogene Cetacea of the World
    • Authorizer: Mark Uhen
    • Enterers: Nick Pyenson, Mark Uhen
    • To download the data: Search for oldest time interval = Miocene and youngest time interval = Holocene and taxon = Cetacea
  • Late Paleocene - early Eocene macrofloras of southwestern Wyoming 2000
    • Authorizer: Peter Wilf
    • Enterer: Peter Wilf
    • Primary reference: P. Wilf. 2000. Late Paleocene-early Eocene climate changes in southwestern Wyoming: Paleobotanical analysis. Geological Society of America Bulletin 112(2):292-307
    • To download the data: Search for authorizer = P. Wilf and time interval = Tertiary and state = Wyoming
