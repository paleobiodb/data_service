Plant organ tip sheet
Important: do not assign a value to any fossil that is not a plant. Instead, leave the pulldown blank.

  • Unassigned

    Plant material that can't be assigned to any of the following categories because no information is given.

  • Plant debris

    Material that is clearly part of a terrestrial plant but cannot be assigned to any of the following categories because of poor preservation, maceration, etc.

  • Marine palynomorph

    Reproductive or somatic microfossil of algal, dinoflagellate, or equivalent affinity.

  • Microspore

    Small haploid spore, roughly < 200 micrometers in equatorial diameter (includes pollen, microspores, and isospores).

  • Megaspore

    Large haploid spore, roughly > 200 micrometers in equatorial diameter.

  • Seed/fruit

    Seed plant female reproductive structure (includes ovules and preovules).

  • Flower

    Angiosperm reproductive axis (includes male, female or bisexual flowers).

  • Seed plant reproductive structure.

    Compound structure bearing ovules or pollen, including things like cones, scales, etc., but which cannot be placed in "ovules" or "flowers."

  • Non-seed plant reproductive structure

    Sporangia or compound structure bearing micro- or megaspores.

  • Wood

    Fragments of secondary xylem that are not part of a larger morphological structure.

  • Axis

    Structure clearly forming a trunk, branch, or stem.

  • Root

    Subterranean axial structure (includes roots and rhizomes). If you are not certain that the structure was subterranean, use "axis."

  • Leaf

    Any planar foliar structure regardless of botanical affinity, including petioles.

  • Cuticle

    Unaltered or altered surficial foliar covering, NOT associated with underlying cells. If cells are present, use "leaf."
